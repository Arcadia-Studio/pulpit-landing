What Pulpit does

Ask any sermon-prep question in plain English. Pulpit searches a curated corpus of sermons, commentaries, and primary sources, then gives you a direct answer with citations to the source text.

No hallucination. No generic ChatGPT theology. Every claim is traceable to a real document you can cite from the pulpit.

Try asking things like:

  • Find me three expository sermons on Romans 8:28 from Reformed pastors before 1900.
  • What did the early church fathers say about the doctrine of predestination?
  • Give me five sermon illustrations about forgiveness that aren't trite or overused.
  • Summarize the difference between sanctification in Wesleyan vs. Reformed traditions.

How it works

  1. Ask any question

    Natural language, theological depth. Ask the way you'd ask a seasoned colleague who's read more than you have.

  2. Get grounded answers

    Pulpit retrieves from a real corpus of sermons and theological sources, then synthesizes a direct answer. Every paragraph cites its source.

  3. Cite with confidence

    Click any citation to read the original passage in context. No more wondering if the AI made it up. It didn't.
